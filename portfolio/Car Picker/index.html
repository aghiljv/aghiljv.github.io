Car Picker


1. Technologies used

  1. Angular
  2. CSS3
  3. HTML5

2. Problem

The traditional way of customer choosing a car for himself has been in place for a long time. Most of these methods focusses on the budget, which often results in a bad experience. Our team was eager to jump in and think of another solution. Our cross functional team went through aggressive research and thought process to come up with an application that will choose a car for the customer rather than concentrating on the budget but on the lifestyle.



3. Challenges

In order to make this happen, the level of collaboration required was the likes of which most of the team has never tried before. The backend as well as the machine learning algorithm required a lot of data. It had to be as accurate as possible. But this one also needed a very interactive front-end that pulls and involves the user.



4. Solution

The data collection and backend was done by the respective members of the team. The UX team was phenominal as well the team manager. Everybody pitched in their ideas, every opinion mattered. The Designs provided where then brought to life by me and my fellow developers as was our role in the team. We built the UI and got the results using Angular, CSS3 and HTML5.



5. Result

Although this project was challenging, it helped the team with their cross-functional collaboration and the business was not only impressed but encouraged more applications like this in the future from us.
